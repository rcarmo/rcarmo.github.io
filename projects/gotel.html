Why it exists

Motivation

OpenTelemetry is the right standard for observability but the official Go SDK pulls in a large dependency tree. For small tools and homelab services I wanted just the parts I actually use: span creation, metric recording, and OTLP export.

How it works

Architecture

A thin wrapper over the OTel Go API that initialises a TracerProvider and MeterProvider with sensible defaults, exports via OTLP/gRPC or OTLP/HTTP, and provides a handful of helpers for common patterns (HTTP middleware, goroutine spans, etc.).

Notable features

Highlights

Minimal OTel setup in five lines of Go
Supports both OTLP/gRPC and OTLP/HTTP exporters
Used across the Go terminal family (webterm, go-rdp, go-te) for tracing
